## Competitor Deep Dives

### 1. Ahrefs
- **Price:** $129-449/mo (no free tier, $29 for Webmaster Tools only)
- **Target:** SEO professionals, agencies, enterprise
- **Strengths:** Best backlink database, powerful Site Explorer, accurate data
- **Weaknesses for SMBs:**
  - No free tier (Webmaster Tools is limited)
  - Interface assumes SEO expertise
  - No "what to do next" guidance
  - No local SEO focus
  - No social media or AI visibility
- **G2 Rating:** 4.5/5 (but reviews from SMBs complain about complexity)
- **SMB Quote:** "Ahrefs is like giving a Ferrari to someone who just got their learner's permit."

### 2. Semrush
- **Price:** $139-499/mo (free tier is very limited)
- **Target:** Marketing agencies, mid-market, enterprise
- **Strengths:** All-in-one marketing suite, competitive research, 55+ tools
- **Weaknesses for SMBs:**
  - Overwhelming number of features
  - Steep learning curve (their own academy has 100+ courses)
  - Expensive for single-site owners
  - No done-for-you option
  - Reports are data-heavy, not action-oriented
- **G2 Rating:** 4.5/5
- **SMB Quote:** "I spent more time learning Semrush than actually improving my SEO."

### 3. Moz Pro
- **Price:** $99-599/mo (30-day free trial)
- **Target:** Mid-market, some small business
- **Strengths:** Good educational content, Domain Authority metric, user-friendly
- **Weaknesses for SMBs:**
  - Falling behind Ahrefs/Semrush in data quality
  - Still $99/mo minimum
  - Limited local SEO features
  - No social media or AI visibility
- **G2 Rating:** 4.3/5
- **SMB Quote:** "Moz was great 5 years ago. Now it feels like they're coasting."

### 4. Ubersuggest
- **Price:** $29-99/mo (free tier available)
- **Target:** Small businesses, bloggers, solo entrepreneurs
- **Strengths:** Affordable, Neil Patel brand recognition, simple interface
- **Weaknesses for SMBs:**
  - Data accuracy is questionable (many Reddit complaints)
  - Limited features compared to premium tools
  - No multi-channel (Google only)
  - No done-for-you option
  - Aggressive upselling
- **G2 Rating:** 4.0/5
- **SMB Quote:** "Ubersuggest told me I had 0 backlinks. Ahrefs found 47. I don't trust the data."

### 5. SE Ranking
- **Price:** $55-239/mo (14-day trial)
- **Target:** Small to mid-size businesses, agencies
- **Strengths:** Good value, white-label reports, decent feature set
- **Weaknesses for SMBs:**
  - Still complex for true beginners
  - No free tier
  - Limited brand recognition
  - No AI visibility or social media audit
- **G2 Rating:** 4.6/5
- **SMB Quote:** "Best value SEO tool, but I still had to watch YouTube tutorials to understand it."

### 6. Local Falcon
- **Price:** $25-100/mo
- **Target:** Local businesses tracking Google Maps rankings
- **Strengths:** Best local rank tracker, visual map, affordable
- **Weaknesses for SMBs:**
  - Only does one thing (local rank tracking)
  - No website audit
  - No action plan
  - No social or AI visibility
- **G2 Rating:** 4.7/5
- **SMB Quote:** "Great for seeing where I rank on the map. But it doesn't tell me how to rank higher."

## Market Gaps Goodly Exploits

1. **The "I don't know SEO" gap** — Every tool assumes you know SEO. Goodly translates everything to business outcomes.

2. **The "show me the money" gap** — No tool connects SEO fixes to revenue impact. Goodly estimates lost revenue and potential gains.

3. **The "I need everything in one place" gap** — Businesses need Google + Maps + Social + AI visibility. No single tool covers all five.

4. **The "I want to pay someone to do it" gap** — Self-serve tools stop at self-serve. Goodly offers Concierge for those who want it done for them.

5. **The "free → paid" gap** — Most tools have no free tier or a useless one. Goodly's free tier is genuinely useful (3 real audits/month).

6. **The "local business" gap** — National SEO tools ignore local. Goodly has Google Business Profile audit built in.
