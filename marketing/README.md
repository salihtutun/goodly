## Key Findings

### 1. Small businesses don't want SEO tools — they want to be found
Every Reddit thread, every review, every interview says the same thing: business owners don't care about "domain authority" or "backlink profiles." They care about one thing: **"Is my phone ringing more?"**

### 2. The market is split into two extremes
- **$100+/mo professional tools** (Ahrefs, Semrush, Moz) — too expensive and complex
- **$25-50/mo budget tools** (Ubersuggest, Local Falcon) — too limited or inaccurate
- **Nothing in between that's both affordable AND comprehensive**

### 3. The #1 requested feature doesn't exist
"No tool tells me how much money I'm losing by not ranking. If you could say 'fixing this will bring in approximately $500/month in new customers,' I'd pay for that instantly." — Reddit user

### 4. Local SEO is the biggest underserved market
76% of local searches result in a visit within 24 hours. Yet most SEO tools focus on national/global rankings. Local businesses are desperate for Google Maps and Google Business Profile help.

### 5. "Done-for-you" is a massive untapped market
Many small business owners would pay $500-1,500/month for someone to just handle it. But the gap between "$49 self-serve tool" and "$2,000/month agency" is enormous. Goodly's $1,000 Concierge plan fills this gap perfectly.
